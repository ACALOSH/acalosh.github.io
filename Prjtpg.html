AirRespeck Phone App

The Centre for Speckled Computing is a research group based at the University of Edinburgh. Their team has developed bluetooth heart rate and air pollution sensors that have been used glpbally for partnered research projects with unversities such as Imperial, ITT in Dehli and Harvard. For clinicians and users to interact with said sensors a phone application was created called the AirRespeck App. However this app was poorly deisgned and many people found it difficult to use, which made it hard to collect data properly. I was hired to redesign the UX and UI of the app as well as re-program of the entire phone application itself.

Menu

The menu should give indication of what kind of actions can be done.
-Menu sections/ titles not clear
-No data shown for projects using Airspeck
-Additional activities not shown in menu

Subject Mode

> Subject facing side of the app that should allow patients to see wether their devices are connected to the phone and the status of their devices. Majority of subjects are older in age so accesiblity is heavily considered.
-No clear user story
-Not clear that this is “Subject mode”
-Buttons not consistent, icons not descriptive enough

Supervisor Mode

This is the home page for clinicians monitoring patients to see data and calibrate their devices. There is a lot of information to display so it should be clearly organised.
-No clear user story
-Not clear that this is “Supervisor mode”
-Actions too hidden in menu
-Two different menus being used
-Pairing information isn’t avaliable in Supervisor mode
-Data not labeled/not clear

I became aware of a few design challenges present while working on the airrespeck app and decided to partner with speckled computing for my dissertation.

Poetica Website

For a masters course I was tasked with creating a dynamic website that would explore social spaces in Edinburgh. I decided to make a website that would allow users to create, collaborate and share fridge magnet poetry. The words generated for users are dependent on the time and weather in edinburgh as a way to emphasise the way our environment influences our creative processes.
